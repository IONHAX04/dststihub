About Us

Sona Group of Institutions has been established a century before, founded by Karumuttu Thiagaraj Chettiar. Focusing to lead and give a great transformative educational experience for student in India and foreign countries. He saw the plight of workers in the plantations and fought for their liberation. His idea of liberation was to set people free and even to a higher level of setting minds free through education. He set this idea in motion by establishing Thiagarajar Engineering College in Madurai, and Thiagarajar Polytechnic College, Salem, and sowed the seeds of quality technical education in India. Those seeds now stand as a global brand called ‘Sona’ whose roots run deep into education, textile, IT, research, manufacturing, outsourcing, logistics and livelihood of the communities in large.

About STI Hub

Department of Science and Technology, Ministry of Science and Technology, Govt. of India has the vision to establish Science Technology and Innovation (STI) Hubs to develop, nurture and ensure the development, improvement, and delivery of appropriate and relevant S&T approaches for Creation of Sustainable Livelihoods and enhancing the Quality of Life of the Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe population in the country.

DST has sanctioned Rs.2.77 crores to Sona College of Technology for the project titled “Science Technology and Innovation Hub for livelihood enhancement of SC community in Omalur and Kadayampatti Blocks”. The project duration is 3 years.

Objective of this Project

Objective of the project

  • To improve the Socio-economic status of SC community through Science and Technology Interventions
  • To provide the Marketing and Entrepreneurial Skills Developmental programme for SC community.

Expected benefits of the target groups / population

  • Establishing STI-HUB(A common Facility centre) for the SC community.
  • Training programmes to chrysanthemum farmers on farm mechanization practices.
  • Workshops and training programmes on Coir pith composting technology and model farms to the SC farmers.
  • Training programmes to SC women for generating additional income.
  • Infrastructure for women to generate income and to establish new rural enterprises.
  • Awareness campaigns, capacity building sessions on millet processing and producing value added products
  • MSME Training of Trainer programmes and workshops to unemployed youth and women.

Intervention

Coir Pith Composting for Chrysanthemum cultivation

The proportion of mixing coir pith, cow dung, cattle litter and Pleurotus sajor-caju is done in collaboration with KVK, Santhiyur, Salem.

The benefits of using cow dung and litter is that the cattle waste will help for speedy decomposition, higher yields and better-quality cultivation.

Flower Oil extraction from Chrysanthemum

The proposed intervention is an oil extracting method from chrysanthemum flowers.

Using Traditional Distillation process, increase its economic value addition, with wide market demand product and to enhance the value chain.

Technology used in Millet processing

Reduces drudgery in millet processing and ensure grading of millets based on size. Based on the grading, further the millets will be taken to market or used for value addition.

Value addition of Millets

Pasta, Macroni, Noodles, Vermicelli and Rava will be made as value added products of Millet which will be marketed in favour of the Farmers.

Marketing technology

All the technologies suggested for the target people will be disseminated and representatives from SHGS/FIGS/FPOs and potential youth of the community will be identified and formed a Project Representative Team (PRT). Based on Training of trainers' model, this group will make the proposed solutions sustainable.
